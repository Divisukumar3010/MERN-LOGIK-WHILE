Ratan Naval Tata

Indian industrialist, philanthropist, and former chairman of Tata Group (1937–2024)

Early Life

Ratan Tata was born on 28 December 1937 in Bombay (now Mumbai), India. He was the son of Naval Tata and Sooni Commissariat. He studied architecture at Cornell University and later completed the Advanced Management Program at Harvard Business School.

Career

Ratan Tata joined the Tata Group in 1961 and became Chairman in 1991. Under his leadership, Tata Group transformed into a global powerhouse, acquiring brands like Tetley, Jaguar Land Rover, and Corus Steel. He retired in 2012 but briefly returned as interim chairman in 2016.

Top Awards

Padma Bhushan

India’s third-highest civilian award, received in 2000.

Padma Vibhushan

India’s second-highest civilian award, received in 2008.

Order of Australia

Received in 2023 for strengthening India–Australia relations and global leadership.

Philanthropy

Ratan Tata was known for his humility and generosity. Through the Tata Trusts, he supported education, healthcare, rural development, and innovation. He played a key role in initiatives like cancer treatment centers, scholarships for Indian students abroad, and disaster relief efforts.

Legacy

Ratan Tata passed away on 9 October 2024 at the age of 86. He is remembered as a visionary leader who combined business excellence with compassion. His legacy continues to inspire entrepreneurs, leaders, and citizens across India and the world.
